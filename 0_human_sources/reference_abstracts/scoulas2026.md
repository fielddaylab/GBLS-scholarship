## Abstract

This paper presents the findings from a survey distributed at two academic institutions,exploring undergraduates’ campus engagement, self-regulated learning, anddefinitions of academic success. The library was the most frequently visited campusservice, and students used it for both academic and nonacademic activities. Studentsidentified obtaining good grades as their top definition of academic success.Participation in library and campus activities, use of online library resources, sociallearning, and improved concentration were positively associated. These findingshighlight the crucial role of academic libraries and their potential for collaborationwith other campus units to support student success
