## Abstract

Abstract: As a graduate student at Brown University and as the 2020–22 Native American and Indigenous Studies Initiative (NAISI) fellow, the author was charged with the task to help the John Hay Special Collections Library implement the Protocols for Native American Archival Materials. The purpose of the Protocols is to provide best professional practices related to culturally responsive care and use of Native American archival materials primarily within non-Native institutions. This article reflects on that work applying the Protocols to the John Hay Library. This article elucidates how the charge to implement these Protocols took shape and describes the logic behind the forms to show other Native and non-Native practitioners one approach toward applying the Protocols.
