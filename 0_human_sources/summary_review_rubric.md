# Summary Review Rubric

A 5-point rubric for evaluating whether an article summary is suitable for use in the Games-Based Library Services literature review. Each dimension is scored from 1 (Poor) to 5 (Excellent).

Rubric ID: `gbls_summary_quality`  
Version: `2.0.0`

# Factual Accuracy

How faithfully does the summary represent the source?

- 1 (Poor): Contains major inaccuracies, misrepresents key claims, or includes information not supported by the source.
- 2 (Fair): Includes some accurate information but contains notable errors, distortions, or misleading interpretations.
- 3 (Adequate): Generally accurate, though minor inaccuracies or omissions may be present. Core claims are represented correctly.
- 4 (Good): Accurately represents the source with only trivial errors or ambiguities. Key claims and evidence are faithfully conveyed.
- 5 (Excellent): Fully accurate and faithful to the source. No substantive errors, distortions, or unsupported claims.

# Coverage & Completeness

Does the summary capture the source's important purpose, methods, findings, and implications?

- 1 (Poor): Omits most major elements of the source and provides an incomplete or misleading picture.
- 2 (Fair): Captures some important elements but misses several key aspects of the purpose, methods, findings, or implications.
- 3 (Adequate): Includes the main points of the source but lacks important details or nuance in one or more areas.
- 4 (Good): Covers nearly all major elements and provides a well-rounded representation of the source.
- 5 (Excellent): Thoroughly captures the source's purpose, methods, findings, and implications with appropriate detail and balance.

# Clarity & Usefulness

Is the summary readable and useful for synthesis?

- 1 (Poor): Difficult to understand, poorly organized, or not useful for informing further analysis or synthesis.
- 2 (Fair): Somewhat understandable but contains organizational, readability, or coherence issues that limit usefulness.
- 3 (Adequate): Clear enough to understand and generally useful, though improvements in organization, conciseness, or emphasis would help.
- 4 (Good): Well-written, logically organized, and useful for synthesis or literature review purposes.
- 5 (Excellent): Exceptionally clear, concise, and well-structured; highly useful for comparison, synthesis, and decision-making.
