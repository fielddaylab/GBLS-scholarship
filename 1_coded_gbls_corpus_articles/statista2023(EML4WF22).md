## Findings
**Market Revenue:** 
- Global board game revenue peaked at $8.3 billion in 2023, forecast to reach $9.9 billion by 2028. Average revenue per capita reached $1.08 in 2023, projected to hit $1.24 by 2028.
- Global card game revenue was estimated at $3.27 billion for 2028, showing continuous growth from 2019 levels. Average revenue per capita for card games is projected to reach $0.41 by 2028.
- The United States leads both markets in revenue ($2.61 billion for board games; $817.7 million for card games in 2023), followed by China and Japan.

**Product Popularity:** 
- On BoardGameGeek (Sept 2024), *Brass: Birmingham* had the highest rating (8.41/10). *Ark Nova* was the most played game (>100,000 plays Jan–Sept 2024).
- On Kickstarter (Jan/Sept 2024), *Frosthaven* was the most funded project (~$13 million), while *Exploding Kittens* had the most backers (~220,000).
- In the U.S. (May 2023), Solitaire was the most popular card game (89% liked/loved it; 83% had played it).

**Demographics and Engagement:** 
- Globally (Sept 2023), Belgium showed the highest interest in board/card games (31%), followed by the U.S. (23%).
- Global board game enthusiasts were nearly evenly split by gender (51% female, 49% male). The largest age group was 18–34 years old (47%), while those 55+ comprised only 18%.
- In the U.S. (Q3 2023), women reported higher liking of board games (72%) compared to men (59%). Millennials (66%) and Gen X (68%) showed slightly higher interest than Baby Boomers (65%).
- Only 24% of U.S. adults considered board/card games a hobby in Q1 2024, ranking it 10th among hobbies. In-person card game play was infrequent for most: 49% played "not very often," and 22% "never."

# Summary

This Statista report (2023) provides a detailed statistical analysis of the global and United States markets for board games and card games. The primary objective is to quantify market size, growth trajectories, leading products, and consumer demographics through aggregated market data, platform metrics, and survey results.

**Methodology and Data Sources**
The report synthesizes data from multiple sources: Statista Market Insights for revenue forecasts (2018–2028), YouGov surveys for consumer preferences and demographics (sample sizes ranging from ~1,000 to 60,327 respondents depending on the quarter and region), BoardGameGeek for user-generated ratings and play counts, and Kickstarter for crowdfunding metrics. The population includes adults aged 18 and older globally, with specific breakdowns for the U.S., China, Japan, Germany, the UK, Belgium, Sweden, Australia, India, Saudi Arabia, and Indonesia.

**Key Findings: Market Economics**
The global board game market demonstrated significant growth, peaking at $8.3 billion in revenue in 2023. Forecasts indicate continued expansion, with revenue expected to reach $9.9 billion by 2028. Average revenue per capita rose from $0.84 in 2018 to $1.08 in 2023, projected to hit $1.24 by 2028. The United States dominates this sector with $2.61 billion in revenue, followed by China ($1.175 billion) and Japan ($465 million). Similarly, the card game market is expanding, with global revenue forecasted to reach $3.27 billion by 2028. The U.S. leads this segment as well, generating $817.7 million in 2023, significantly outpacing China ($408.16 million) and other nations.

**Key Findings: Product Popularity**
Platform data from BoardGameGeek (as of September 2024) identifies *Brass: Birmingham* as the highest-rated board game (8.41/10), while *Ark Nova* was the most played, with over 100,000 recorded plays between January and September 2024. In the crowdfunding space, *Frosthaven* raised approximately $13 million on Kickstarter, and *Exploding Kittens* secured nearly 220,000 backers. In the U.S., Solitaire remains the most popular card game, with 89% of respondents expressing positive sentiment and 83% reporting prior play experience.

**Key Findings: Demographics and Engagement**
Demographic analysis reveals that board gaming is a widely distributed hobby. Globally, interest is highest in Belgium (31%) and the U.S. (23%). The gender split among global enthusiasts is nearly even (51% female, 49% male). However, age distribution is skewed younger: 47% of enthusiasts are aged 18–34, compared to only 18% aged 55+. In the U.S., women report higher affinity for board games (72%) than men (59%). Millennials (66%) and Gen X (68%) show slightly higher interest than Baby Boomers (65%). Despite this popularity, only 24% of U.S. adults classified board/card games as a hobby in Q1 2024, ranking it 10th among all hobbies. Furthermore, active participation in card games is low: 49% of U.S. respondents play in-person card games "not very often," and 22% "never."

**Implications for Library Services**
The data underscores a large, growing market with a diverse demographic base, particularly strong among younger adults and women. The discrepancy between high familiarity (e.g., Solitaire) and low active participation suggests that libraries may need to focus on lowering barriers to entry or creating social structures to convert passive interest into active play. The dominance of the U.S. market and the success of specific titles like *Ark Nova* and *Exploding Kittens* provide concrete recommendations for collection development.
