## Findings
The authors categorize UGC into several formats: comments, reviews, overviews, creative designs/drawings, photographs, videos, ratings/comparisons, podcasts, and NFTs. They identify five key components for a successful gamified UGC strategy: (1) an idea/format aligned with audience interests; (2) a thoughtful reward (material or immaterial, such as reposting); (3) a clear call-to-action (CTA); (4) an appropriate platform for publishing; and (5) careful selection of content/winners to maintain reputation. The study highlights that gamification mechanics—such as interactive contests, visual AR filters, quizzes, competitive status awards, and creative collaborations—are effective in motivating users to create content. Case studies demonstrate that campaigns like Starbucks’ #WhiteCupContest generated nearly 4,000 entries in three weeks, illustrating the viral potential of gamified UGC. The authors conclude that UGC acts as social proof, increasing trust and engagement for libraries.

# Summary

This article by Maranchak and Maranchak (2023) explores the application of gamification mechanics in internet marketing for libraries, specifically focusing on stimulating User-Generated Content (UGC). The authors posit that in the digital environment of Web 2.0, traditional advertising is often ignored by users fatigued by direct promotion. Consequently, UGC serves as a critical tool for building trust and organic reach, functioning as a form of digital word-of-mouth marketing.

The study utilizes qualitative methods, including bibliographic analysis, content analysis, and selective observation of internet resources. The data comprises an examination of official social media accounts of Ukrainian libraries and comparative case studies from international commercial brands such as Starbucks, Lay’s, and Mercedes-Benz. The authors do not conduct a quantitative experiment with human subjects; instead, they analyze digital artifacts—posts, comments, videos, and campaigns—to derive best practices for library marketing.

The findings categorize UGC into various formats, including comments, reviews, creative designs, photographs, videos, ratings, podcasts, and NFTs. The authors argue that gamification—the application of game thinking and dynamics to non-game contexts—can effectively motivate users to create this content. They propose a five-component strategy for launching successful gamified UGC campaigns: (1) an idea and format that align with the target audience’s interests; (2) a thoughtful reward system, which can be material (gifts, discounts) or immaterial (reposting on official pages, emotional satisfaction); (3) a clear call-to-action (CTA) with visual cues and deadlines; (4) an appropriate platform for content placement; and (5) rigorous selection criteria to ensure quality and reputation management.

The article highlights several types of gamification mechanics applicable to libraries: interactive (contests, challenges), visual (AR filters, stickers), inquisitive (quizzes, polls), competitive (status awards), and creative/social (co-creation). It cites the Starbucks #WhiteCupContest as a prime example, which generated nearly 4,000 entries in three weeks through a simple gamified prompt. Similarly, it references Ukrainian examples like Lay’s #SmileUA campaign. The authors also discuss offline-to-online gamification, such as art installations in libraries (e.g., the Foucault pendulum at NTUU "Igor Sikorsky Kyiv Polytechnic Institute") that encourage users to take photos and tag the institution.

The implications for library services are significant. The authors recommend that librarians adopt these marketing strategies to enhance user engagement and attract new patrons. By leveraging gamification, libraries can transform passive users into active brand ambassadors who voluntarily share positive experiences. The study concludes that while gamification is one of many tools, its proven effectiveness in commercial sectors makes it a necessary component of modern library internet marketing strategies, helping to strengthen relationships with existing clients and expand the user base through social proof.
