# Summary

The Newzoo Global Games Market Report 2025 provides a comprehensive analysis of the video game industry’s current state and future trajectory through 2028. The report is grounded in a hybrid methodology that combines top-down demographic modeling with bottom-up survey data from over 73,000 respondents, triangulated against proprietary engagement metrics and public financial reports. The primary objective is to offer strategic benchmarks for market performance, highlighting shifts in player behavior, revenue distribution, and platform dynamics.

**Market Overview and Growth**
The global games market is characterized by moderate but stable growth. In 2025, the total player base is projected to reach 3.6 billion, a 4.4% year-over-year increase, representing 61.5% of the global online population. However, the share of the online population playing games is plateauing, indicating market maturity. Total global revenue is forecast to reach $188.8 billion, a 3.4% increase. A notable trend is that payer growth (4.9%) is outpacing total player growth and revenue growth, resulting in a slight decline in average spend per payer. This suggests a shift toward broader monetization of existing audiences rather than acquiring new high-spending users.

**Platform Dynamics**
Mobile gaming remains the dominant sector, accounting for 83% of all players (3.0 billion) and 55% of total revenue ($103.0 billion). While mobile player growth is strong in emerging regions, growth in mature markets like the US and Europe is slowing due to economic pressures and saturated iOS markets. PC gaming shows renewed momentum, with player growth (3.1%) outpacing console growth (2.5%). Steam players are expanding rapidly, particularly in China and Japan. Console revenue, however, is projected to grow the fastest at 5.5%, driven by the launch of the Nintendo Switch 2, higher software price points, and major title releases.

**Strategic Insights: Release Timing and Early Access**
A significant portion of the report analyzes data from single-player titles released between 2021 and 2024 to determine optimal release strategies. The findings challenge traditional industry wisdom regarding holiday releases. Data indicates that titles released in the first half of the year (February–May) achieve 34% higher average player counts in their first three months compared to those released in the crowded second half (August–November). The latter period suffers from sales cannibalization as publishers compete for limited wallet share.

Regarding Early Access, the report identifies a "sweet spot" of approximately six months. Titles that transition from Early Access to full release within 4–9 months demonstrate the highest lift in new player acquisition at v1.0. Longer Early Access periods often fail to re-activate players effectively and may diminish launch momentum.

**Platform Exclusivity and Attrition**
The analysis of platform exclusivity reveals that simultaneous multi-platform launches are superior for player acquisition compared to staggered ports. For instance, when PlayStation exclusives are later ported to PC, the PC version captures only about 13% of the total players across both platforms. In contrast, simultaneous releases yield a more balanced distribution (approximately 60/40 split).

Player attrition patterns show that engagement drops sharply between weeks 2 and 5 post-launch, losing nearly 60% of the initial player base before stabilizing. The report suggests that games with main story lengths between 21 and 40 hours exhibit the best retention during this critical window. Longer titles (80+ hours) only excel in long-tail retention if they feature simulation or management elements.

**Implications for Library Services and Industry Strategy**
For stakeholders, including those involved in Games-Based Library Services (GBLS), these findings underscore the importance of timing and platform availability. The data supports strategies that prioritize immediate accessibility across platforms to maximize reach. Furthermore, the shift toward moderate growth and retention-focused monetization suggests that library services should focus on sustaining engagement with existing gaming communities rather than expecting explosive new user acquisition. The report also highlights the growing significance of Gen Alpha and the Roblox ecosystem, noting that creator-driven platforms are becoming foundational for reaching younger demographics.
