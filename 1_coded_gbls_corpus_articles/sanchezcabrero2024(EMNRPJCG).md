## Findings
The review found that leveraging Lore in formal education is feasible but requires significant initial production effort and cost. The six identified studies demonstrated positive outcomes, including increased motivation to read (Mora-Cantallops & Sicilia, 2016), the development of prosocial values through collective meaning-making (Musabirov et al., 2017), and effective tangential learning in history (Anderson, 2019). The analysis revealed strong structural similarities between Lore and modern educational methodologies like Connectivism, Universal Design for Learning (UDL), Multi-level Teaching, Student-Centered Learning, Personal Learning Environments (PLEs), Learning Landscapes, Design Thinking, Problem-Based Learning (PBL), Challenge-Based Learning (CBL), and the Maker Movement. Key shared attributes include personalization, autonomy, optional engagement, and non-linear progression. However, Lore differs from gamification in its lack of explicit progress tracking and from serious games in its open-ended nature.
Evidence: "Se concluyó que el aprovechamiento del Lore desde la educación formal es posible, aunque supone un gran esfuerzo y coste de producción inicial" (Abstract); "El aprendizaje a través del Lore se activa principalmente por exposición al contenido y se enriquece a voluntad del aprendiz por mera motivación intrínseca" (Discussion on Connectivism).

# Summary

This article by Roberto Sánchez-Cabrero (2024) investigates the potential of "Lore"—the intricate, secondary narratives and world-building elements characteristic of video games—as a resource for formal education. The study addresses whether trends in digital leisure can be successfully translated into pedagogical tools, similar to how gamification and serious games have been adopted. The primary objective is to evaluate the feasibility of using Lore in educational settings by reviewing existing academic literature and identifying structural parallels between Lore and contemporary educational methodologies.

The methodology employed is a theoretical review following the PRISMA protocol for systematic literature exploration. Searches were conducted in October 2023 across Web of Science and Scopus databases using specific Boolean combinations related to "Lore," "video games," and "education." From an initial pool of 29 records, six publications published between 2016 and 2023 were selected for detailed analysis based on their explicit connection between video game Lore and educational application. Additionally, the author conducted a broader review of ludic-didactic methodologies (gamification, game-based learning, serious games) to understand precedents for cross-disciplinary adaptation.

The findings indicate that while only six studies have explicitly addressed this topic, the results are promising. Key empirical insights from the reviewed literature include: Mora-Cantallops and Sicilia (2016) found that Lore in *League of Legends* significantly motivated users to read and deepen their knowledge; Musabirov et al. (2017) demonstrated that Lore in *Dota 2* facilitated the construction of collective values and prosocial behaviors; Anderson (2019) highlighted the potential of Lore for tangential history learning through "interactive museums"; and Landa and Thompson (2023) showed how Lore can foster interest in archaeology. The qualitative analysis revealed strong affinities between Lore and modern educational paradigms such as Connectivism, Universal Design for Learning (UDL), Multi-level Teaching, Student-Centered Learning, Personal Learning Environments (PLEs), Learning Landscapes, Design Thinking, Problem-Based Learning (PBL), Challenge-Based Learning (CBL), and the Maker Movement. These methodologies share core principles with Lore, including personalization, learner autonomy, optional engagement, and non-linear progression.

The author concludes that integrating Lore into formal education is feasible and beneficial for fostering intrinsic motivation and deep engagement without causing learner reactance. However, this approach presents significant challenges, primarily the high initial cost and effort required for design and production. Unlike traditional methods where content delivery is ongoing, Lore requires upfront investment to embed all potential learning paths. Once established, however, it operates in an "unattended" manner, reducing long-term teacher workload and allowing simultaneous access for multiple students. The article suggests that emerging technologies, particularly artificial intelligence, may lower these barriers in the future. The study positions Lore as a valuable, albeit underexplored, resource for creating inclusive, personalized, and motivating educational experiences, warranting further scientific investigation.
