# Summary

The provided text is a resource document associated with a 2024 GameRT (Games Round Table) session titled "Low Stress High Mayhem Resources: Venture Society," hosted by the American Library Association. The document functions as a curated bibliography and guide for librarians and educators interested in integrating tabletop role-playing games (TTRPGs) into library services, with a specific emphasis on low-stress, non-violent, and child-friendly options.

The primary purpose of the text is to provide practical resources for facilitating TTRPG sessions that prioritize safety and inclusivity. It begins by defining key terminology, including "tabletop roleplaying game," "cozy game," "roleplaying focused game," "non-violent ttrpg," and "kids ttrpg." This terminological framing establishes the scope of the resource list, which aims to move beyond traditional combat-heavy RPGs toward more wholesome and accessible experiences.

The document categorizes recommended games into three distinct groups based on their thematic content and target audience. For children and tweens, it suggests titles such as *Magical Kitties Save the Day* by Atlas Games, *Amazing Tales* by Martin Lloyd, and *Quest* by The Adventure Guild. For players seeking "Redwall Vibes" (implying gentle, nature-focused fantasy), it recommends *Wanderhome* by Possum Creek Games and *Cozy Town* by Rae Nedjadi. For those interested in "Fantastical World Vibes," it lists *Monster Care Squad* by Sandy Pug Games. These recommendations are presented as alternatives to high-stress gaming environments, aligning with the session's title.

A significant portion of the text is dedicated to safety resources, reflecting a critical component of modern library programming involving interactive play. It provides links to the "TTRPG Safety Toolkit" collected by Kienna Shaw and Lauren Bryant-Monk, including a guide and Google Drive access. This underscores the importance of establishing clear boundaries and consent mechanisms in gaming groups, particularly when working with younger or vulnerable populations.

Furthermore, the text bridges recreational gaming with academic research by listing several scholarly articles that examine the benefits of TTRPGs. These include Ruff (2021) on social and emotional learning competencies; Cao et al. (2018) on interpersonal communication; Parks (2022) on social learning among autistic young adults; Taylor (2019) on emotional resiliency; Daniau (2016) on the transformative potential of role-playing games; Glickman (2022) on therapeutic use for LGBTQIA+ individuals; and Bawa (2022) on motivation in educational settings. This collection of literature supports the argument that TTRPGs can serve as effective tools for social-emotional development, therapy, and education, providing a theoretical foundation for their inclusion in library services.

The document also outlines the structure of the associated Twitch stream, which included an introduction to rules, actual play demonstration, and discussion. It concludes with standard community guidelines for the online session, emphasizing kindness, respect, and zero tolerance for hate speech, along with contact information for GameRT. Overall, the text serves as a comprehensive starting point for librarians seeking to implement safe, inclusive, and educationally beneficial TTRPG programs, supported by both practical game recommendations and relevant academic literature.
