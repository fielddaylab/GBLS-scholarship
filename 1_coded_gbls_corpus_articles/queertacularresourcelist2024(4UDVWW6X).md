### Summary
# **Queertacular Resource List. (2024). *Google Docs*. https://docs.google.com/document/d/1TZ8Ub6hqYjbFaD0LO6LBwxxZvHcsJRuxMcJk0WA5in4/edit?usp=sharing&usp=embed_facebook**

This item is a nine-page crowd-sourced Queertacular resource list recommended by members of several ALA round tables: the Film & Media Round Table, Games & Gaming Round Table, Graphic Novels & Comics Round Table, and Map and Geospatial Information Round Table. It accompanies a Twitch-broadcast event and includes a survey link, recording link, GameRT Twitch policy, schedule of events, queer media recommendations, and round table contact information.

The event schedule includes a round table discussion about queer representation and creators across media, a dramatic comics reading, a film screening and director chat, a queer geographies segment with an interactive Worldle-style game, and a GameRT-led liveplay of *Yazeba's Bed & Breakfast*. The resource list then organizes recommendations across books, comics, film and television, games, maps and geospatial information, actual plays, archives, zines, magazines, awards, newsletters, and gaming/geek-culture publications.

The games section is directly relevant as a recommendation trail for queer game collections and programming. It lists tabletop, digital, and role-playing games such as *Artisans of Splendent Vale*, *Celeste*, *Coffee Talk*, *Dream Askew*, *Legacy of Dragonholt*, *Life is Strange: True Colors*, *Night in the Woods*, *Our Mundane Supernatural Life*, *The Queer Agenda*, *Romantic*, *Sapphic Space Pirates*, *Stonewall Uprising*, *Thirsty Sword Lesbians*, *Undertale*, *Unpacking*, and *Visigoths vs Mall Goths*. The other-content section adds actual play streams and podcasts, the LGBTQ Video Game Archive, queer zines, the Nonbinary Tabletop Award, Gayming Magazine, and newsletters highlighting games by gender-marginalized creators.

For the larger GBLS project, this item is useful as a professional resource trail for inclusive collection development, advisory, displays, liveplay programming, cross-round-table collaboration, and queer representation across games, comics, film, books, maps, archives, and zines. It also shows GameRT's recurring moderation infrastructure for online events: kindness and respect, no hate speech, moderator authority, harassment reporting, and private communication options.

However, it should not be added to `review.md` as literature evidence. The document does not study library gaming programs, analyze patron outcomes, document a library collection policy, evaluate inclusive game advisory, or describe a specific Queertacular program in enough detail to assess design, staffing, audience, or impact. It is a recommendation list and event handout. If the GBLS project later needs an appendix of inclusive games or examples of queer media programming, this document could be useful, but the main review should rely on sources that make claims about game-based library services and their effects.
