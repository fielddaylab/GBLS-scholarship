# Summary

This Statista report provides a detailed statistical analysis of the global and United States markets for board games and card games, covering revenue trends, leading products, and player demographics. The document aggregates data from 2018 through 2023, with forecasts extending to 2028, drawing from market insights, consumer surveys, and platform metrics.

**Market Overview and Revenue**
The global board game market demonstrated significant growth, peaking at $8.3 billion in revenue in 2023. Statista forecasts this figure to rise to $9.9 billion by 2028. The United States is the dominant market, generating over $2.6 billion in 2023, followed by China ($1.2 billion) and Japan ($465 million). Similarly, the card game segment reached $2.75 billion globally in 2023, with projections of $3.27 billion by 2028. The U.S. leads this sector as well, with $817.7 million in revenue, substantially outpacing China ($408.16 million) and other nations. Average revenue per capita for board games reached $1.08 in 2023, expected to grow to $1.24 by 2028.

**Leading Games and Platforms**
The report identifies top-performing titles through user ratings and crowdfunding success. As of September 2024, *Brass: Birmingham* held the highest rating on BoardGameGeek (8.41/10), while *Ark Nova* was the most frequently played game among users, with over 108,000 recorded plays between January and September 2024. In the crowdfunding space, *Frosthaven* raised approximately $13 million on Kickstarter by January 2024, making it the most funded tabletop project. *Exploding Kittens* secured the highest number of backers (nearly 220,000) as of September 2024. In the U.S., Solitaire remains the most popular card game, with 89% of respondents expressing liking or loving it, and 83% having played it previously.

**Demographics and Engagement**
Global surveys indicate that board game enthusiasts are nearly evenly distributed by gender (51% female, 49% male) but skew significantly younger, with 47% of players aged 18–34 and only 18% aged 55+. In the U.S., interest in board games is consistent across generations, with Millennials (66%) and Gen X (68%) showing slightly higher engagement than Baby Boomers (65%). Approximately 23–24% of U.S. adults consider board or card games one of their hobbies. However, active participation appears lower than passive interest: while 56% of U.S. respondents like playing card games, 49% report not playing in-person "very often," and only 7% play "very often."

**Relevance to Library Services**
For libraries considering games-based services, this data highlights a large, engaged audience, particularly among young adults and Millennials. The high popularity of specific titles like *Ark Nova* and *Solitaire* provides actionable intelligence for collection development. Furthermore, the discrepancy between high familiarity with card games and low frequency of in-person play suggests an opportunity for libraries to facilitate social gaming events, thereby converting passive interest into active community engagement.
