# Summary

This document, sourced from Statista, provides a comprehensive statistical overview of the global video gaming industry, aggregating data from 2019 through forecasts for 2029. The report is structured into eight chapters covering market revenue, audience demographics, user engagement, device usage, mobile gaming specifics, streaming content, purchasing behavior, and eSports.

**Market Overview and Revenue:**
The global video game market demonstrates robust growth. Revenue increased from $285.19 billion in 2019 to an estimated $475.04 billion in 2023, with a forecast peak of $691.31 billion by 2029. Mobile gaming is the dominant segment, generating $91.8 billion in 2022 (50% of the market), followed by console games ($52.2 billion) and PC games ($36.5 billion). Geographically, Asia-Pacific holds the largest market share (46%), followed by North America (27%). China leads individual country revenues at $82.06 billion in 2023, ahead of the United States ($68.27 billion).

**Audience and Demographics:**
The global gamer population is projected to reach 3.02 billion by 2029. As of Q2 2024, 84.1% of internet users worldwide played video games. Penetration rates vary significantly by region, with the Philippines highest (97.1%) and the UK/Japan lowest (~68%). Demographically, gaming is highly prevalent among youth; in Q2 2024, over 90% of both male and female internet users aged 16–24 were gamers. Shooter and action-adventure genres are the most popular across all age groups. Notably, user engagement data indicates that gaming serves psychological functions: in 2023, high percentages of gamers in countries like Brazil (87%) and Spain (75%) reported playing to relieve stress or feel happier.

**Device Usage and Mobile Gaming:**
Smartphones are the primary gaming device globally, used by 70.1% of internet users in Q2 2024, surpassing PCs/laptops (35.8%) and consoles (23.7%). This trend is consistent across surveyed nations, including the U.S., Canada, Brazil, Germany, and China. In mobile gaming, puzzle games are the most played genre (50% of users), while titles like *Honor of Kings* and *Monopoly GO!* lead in revenue generation.

**Purchasing Behavior and eSports:**
Amazon is the preferred store brand for video game purchases in most Western markets (U.S., Canada, Germany, UK, Japan). However, regional variations exist, with Blizzard/Battle.net leading in China and Google Play Store in South Korea. The eSports sector is also expanding rapidly, valued at $1.64 billion in 2022 and forecast to exceed $10 billion by 2032. *League of Legends* remains the most-watched eSports discipline, with over 590 million hours viewed in 2023.

**Relevance to Games-Based Library Services (GBLS):**
While this report does not explicitly address library services, its data supports the viability of GBLS initiatives. The high penetration of gaming across diverse demographics, including older adults and females, suggests a broad potential user base for library-based gaming programs. The documented stress-relief benefits of gaming align with library missions to support community well-being. Furthermore, the dominance of mobile and PC platforms indicates that libraries may not need expensive console infrastructure to engage users, as smartphones are already ubiquitous. The data provides a macro-level context for understanding the scale and nature of the gaming audience that libraries might seek to serve.
