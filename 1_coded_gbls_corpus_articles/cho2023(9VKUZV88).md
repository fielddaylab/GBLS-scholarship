## Findings
The escape room appeared to increase awareness of specific misinformation tactics, particularly deepfakes and manipulated data. Participants reflected on their vulnerability to misinformation, acknowledging cognitive biases like confirmation bias and the third-person effect. The game’s narrative twist—where players unwittingly shared a deepfake video—provoked significant reflection on the consequences of sharing information online. Many participants reported adopting a more cautious attitude toward social media content and expressed intent to verify information before sharing. Librarians noted that the collaborative nature of the game and the post-game debrief facilitated meaningful peer learning and discussion, though they highlighted logistical challenges with online implementation.

# Summary

This article presents an exploratory pilot study evaluating "The Euphorigen Investigation," a misinformation-themed escape room designed to enhance resilience against misinformation through immersive, collaborative gameplay and facilitated reflection. The authors argue that conventional information literacy approaches often fail to address the psychological dimensions of misinformation, such as cognitive biases and emotional triggers. To address this gap, they developed an escape room that avoids didactic skills-building in favor of experiential learning, allowing players to "fail safely" by encountering and sharing misinformation within a fictional context.

The study was conducted across five public libraries in Washington State between February and March 2021. A total of 80 participants engaged in 17 sessions, working in teams of 3–6 players. The intervention consisted of approximately 45–60 minutes of gameplay followed by a 15–20 minute post-game debrief discussion facilitated by trained librarians. Data collection methods included audio-video recordings of gameplay and debriefs, participant surveys, librarian session reports, and a librarian focus group. Qualitative data from video transcripts were analyzed using thematic analysis with paired coders in ATLAS.ti, while quantitative survey data (n=50) were used descriptively.

The findings indicate that the escape room successfully increased participants' awareness of specific misinformation techniques, particularly deepfake images and videos, which many players found novel or alarming. The game’s design intentionally elicited feelings of vulnerability by having players unwittingly share a deepfake video, thereby challenging the "third-person effect" (the belief that others are more susceptible to misinformation than oneself). Participants frequently reflected on their own cognitive biases, such as confirmation bias, during the debrief sessions. Many reported a shift in attitude toward greater caution when encountering and sharing information on social media, with some describing new strategies for verification, such as "vibe checking" or cross-referencing sources.

Librarian feedback highlighted the value of the collaborative format and the post-game debrief in fostering peer learning and civil discourse about controversial topics. However, librarians also noted logistical challenges associated with the online delivery model, including technical difficulties and recruitment issues. They suggested that an in-person version would be more appealing and recommended incorporating real-world examples into future iterations to strengthen the connection between game mechanics and daily life.

The authors conclude that immersive, collaborative games facilitated by trusted community figures like librarians offer a promising avenue for misinformation education. The study underscores the critical role of the post-game debrief in transforming subjective game experiences into transferable knowledge. Limitations include the small sample size, which precludes statistical generalization, and the potential for social desirability bias in self-reported behaviors. The authors recommend further large-scale and longitudinal studies to assess long-term behavioral impacts and to explore adaptations for diverse communities and formats.
