Read `00_shared_section_contract.md`, all preceding section artifacts, and all project inputs.

Write `2-outputs/sections/06_cross_cutting_conditions.md`.

## Required Structure

# Cross-Cutting Conditions for Effective Games-Based Library Services
## Begin with Purpose, Audience, and Local Need
## Match Game Form to Service Goal
## Treat Facilitation as Core Infrastructure
## Curate for Inclusion, Accessibility, and Cultural Relevance
## Connect Discovery, Advisory, and Cross-Media Engagement
## Build Partnerships and Community Leadership Pathways
## Develop Staff Expertise and Professional Preparation
## Plan for Operations, Licensing, Preservation, and Sustainability
## Design for Reflection, Learning Transfer, and Participant Agency
## Evaluate Outcomes Beyond Attendance and Enjoyment

## Target

Approximately 2,250 words.

## Section Task

Derive field-level design and service principles that recur across formats and library types. Make this section the practical analytical center of the review, not a generic checklist.

For each condition, compare supporting evidence, implementation knowledge, tensions, and exceptions. Emphasize community assessment, form-purpose fit, beginner support, conflict management, debriefing, cultural specificity, accessibility, discovery, patron leadership, distributed expertise, platform governance, maintenance, preservation, and assessment aligned to intended outcomes.

Address the danger of services depending on one enthusiast, partnerships that offload institutional responsibility, nominal openness that reproduces gatekeeping, and operations treated as afterthoughts. Distinguish formative evidence during play from summative outcomes and short-term reaction from transfer or durability.

End with a consulting-style synthesis for librarians, directors, and boards: what must be funded, staffed, governed, and evaluated if GBLS is to become durable service infrastructure. Transition to what the evidence still cannot establish.
