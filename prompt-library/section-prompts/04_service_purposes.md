## Section Task

Explain what libraries collectively seek to accomplish through GBLS. Treat access as material, technical, informational, social, cultural, and spatial. Connect collections to hardware, accounts, staff guidance, accessible interfaces, safe groups, and usable spaces.

Synthesize user-centered metadata, game-specific discovery, appeals, relationships, versions, technical dependencies, and cross-media advisory. For instruction, compare orientation, information literacy, misinformation, situated practice, motivation, retention, anxiety, and transfer; do not equate engagement with learning.

Treat belonging and human flourishing as possible primary outcomes while maintaining strict limits on therapeutic claims. Address games as cultural media, Indigenous and culturally situated creation, heritage objects, development artifacts, and preservation relationships. Cover research support and participatory creation across disciplines.

Frame outreach as a pathway into sustained relationships and leadership rather than publicity or foot traffic alone. End by explaining that program formats are delivery models whose value depends on fit with these purposes.
