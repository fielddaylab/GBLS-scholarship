## Section Task

Establish the review's central question: how libraries can engage games as cultural media and forms of social action in ways that advance public access, learning, belonging, stewardship, inquiry, and participation. Frame games as mainstream cultural media without treating market size or educational-game research as direct evidence of library outcomes.

Synthesize:

- The cultural scale and cross-media character of games.
- Libraries as free, locally embedded public knowledge and participation infrastructure.
- The limits of games as responses to misinformation, civic distrust, or fragmented media.
- Historical continuities in library play, toys, puzzles, contests, and nonbook service.
- The recurring tension between experimentation and professional legitimacy.
- Why the fragmented literature needs a field-level synthesis organized around a service ecology.

Use historical work as precedent only. Give national library data and empirical audience evidence more weight than market reports. End by introducing the review's purpose and progression into scope and method.
