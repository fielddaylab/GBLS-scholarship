## Section Task

Compare formats as bundles of affordances, resource demands, risks, and participation structures. Do not make this a parade of case studies. Within each H2, synthesize multiple examples around goals, audience fit, facilitation, continuity, access, operations, evidence strength, and common failure modes.

Explicitly compare:

- High-visibility episodic formats versus recurring relationship-building formats.
- Collections as infrastructure versus events as encounters.
- Competitive, cooperative, narrative, creative, and exploratory participation.
- Low-tech and high-tech resource demands.
- Bounded instructional games versus sustained communities and campaigns.
- Platforms that extend library presence versus platforms that merely replicate a room or website.

Include outliers such as weakly attended pilots, immersive formats that do not outperform conventional instruction, and virtual services with poor audience-platform fit. Close with a cross-format synthesis comparing continuity, facilitation, cost, licensing, accessibility, and differences among public, school, academic, and heritage institutions. Transition to the conditions that determine effectiveness across all models.
