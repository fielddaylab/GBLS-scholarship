# Conduct Two Runtime-Structure Publishability Reviews

Read:

- the current baseline;
- the independently rebuilt runtime structure manifest;
- `0-human-sources/publishability_rubric.md`;
- `2-outputs/SECOND_DRAFT.md`;
- retained working ledgers;
- every file in `2-outputs/audits_and_synthetic_reviews`.

All scores are internal editorial diagnostics, not external peer review or
publication decisions.

## Structural Rule

At the start and end of each round, reread the baseline and rebuild its H1/H2
sequence. Any missing, added, renamed, duplicated, or reordered heading is a
blocking defect. Do not restore a heading from memory or a static prompt.

## Round One

Review `SECOND_DRAFT.md` using every weighted rubric criterion. Infer the
manuscript's section purposes from the runtime baseline. Evaluate contribution,
transparency, thematic synthesis, evidence calibration, cross-context
analysis, equity, practice relevance, research agenda, organization,
baseline-prose handling, corpus coverage, and bidirectional citation
integrity.

Write findings and the score under `# Internal Editorial Review Round One` in
`2-outputs/audits_and_synthetic_reviews/reviewer_notes.md`.

Revise the complete manuscript, update affected audits, and save
`2-outputs/THIRD_DRAFT.md`. Append a concise revision record and revised score.

## Round Two

Review `THIRD_DRAFT.md` independently against the same rubric and current
runtime structure. Identify remaining source-led prose, weak transitions,
unsupported generalizations, missing outliers, repetition, equity blind spots,
citation problems, baseline-traceability problems, and venue-readiness
concerns.

Append findings and the score under
`# Internal Editorial Review Round Two`.

Revise the manuscript again, update affected audits, and save
`2-outputs/FINAL_DRAFT.md`.

Append `# Final Internal Assessment` with the final score, recommendation,
unresolved bibliography items, corpus and baseline-prose caveats, and
venue-specific next steps. State explicitly that the assessment is synthetic
and internal.
