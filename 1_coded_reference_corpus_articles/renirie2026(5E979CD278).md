abstract: Given challenges posed by the COVID-19 pandemic and existing challenges for academic professional associations, the Michigan Health Sciences Library Association (MHSLA) surveyed the leadership of health science library associations in the United States to investigate how smaller state and regional associations engage and add value for members. The survey revealed many respondents are planning to or have returned to the frequency of programming delivered prior to the pandemic and are increasingly providing virtual offerings. These findings will impact the strategic planning for small library associations as they explore ways to recruit and retain membership.
