# Games Are Cultural Media

Games are a major part of contemporary culture. Libraries should treat them with the same seriousness as books, film, music, and other media.

# Libraries Evolve to Meet Public Need

Libraries are dynamic public institutions. They continually adapt their collections, services, spaces, and expertise to meet changing community needs. GBLS fits this tradition because games are now part of how people learn, create, socialize, and participate in culture.

# Libraries Connect People, Not Just Resources

GBLS is not only about access to games. Libraries help people discover, interpret, discuss, create, and learn through games. Collections, advisory, programs, facilitation, and partnerships all support this work.

# Access Must Be Intentional and Inclusive

Access means more than availability. Libraries must consider technology, cost, disability, language, representation, prior experience, safety, belonging, and local context. GBLS should be designed around community needs, not assumptions about who plays.

# Sustainable Services Require Infrastructure

Strong GBLS depends on more than individual programs or enthusiastic staff. It requires curation, staff preparation, facilitation, partnerships, assessment, preservation, policy, and long-term organizational support.
