# **Goudeau, J. (2024). *LSHM: Magikarp Makeover*. Google Docs.**

This short Low Stress High Mayhem handout supports a streamed GameRT session of librarian Jennifer Goudeau's *Magikarp Makeover*, described as a *Honey Heist* hack reminiscent of *Pokemon Mystery Dungeon*. The game is framed as a found-family and hijinks story in which players are Pokemon helping their friend Magikarp find a cool surprise and feel like a hero rather than a zero. The document links to the Twitch VOD and quotes the game's itch.io description, including the journey, other Pokemon, and comic caper premise.

The most distinctive part of the handout is its emphasis on hacking and game creation. Its resource list points to UCLA Game Lab's "Hacking Your First RPG," The Gauntlet material on TTRPG hacks and getting games to the table, a UniversityXP article on easy-to-hack tabletop RPGs, Bloat Games' *Hack the RPG!*, and a *Lasers and Feelings* hacks list. This positions the program not only as a play session but also as an invitation to understand small RPGs as adaptable design systems.

The advisory sections build a wide media pathway around the game. Play-alikes include board and card games such as *Boss Monster*, *King of Tokyo*, *Clank!*, and *Kill Doctor Lucky*; TTRPGs and hacks such as *Honey Heist*, *Goat Crashers*, *The Witch Is Dead*, *Blades in the Dark*, and *Fiasco*; and video games such as *Pokemon Mystery Dungeon*, *Pokemon Go*, and *Undertale*. Read-alikes and watch-alikes connect the game to heist stories, magical adventures, Pokemon media, youth fantasy, found-family stories, and playful caper narratives.

The handout also includes the standard LSHM/GameRT facilitation layer: *Script Change*, lines and veils, *The Door Is Open*, the TTRPG Safety Toolkit, a Twitch policy emphasizing kindness and no hate speech, moderator intervention, harassment reporting, private contact, and GameRT links. These features show how even a lighthearted hack is presented with safety tools, conduct norms, and broader discovery resources.
