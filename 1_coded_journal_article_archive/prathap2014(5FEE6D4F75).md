### Summary
# **Gangan Prathap (2014). Measures for impact, consistency, and the h- and g-indices. *Journal of the Association for Information Science and Technology (JASIST)*, Vol. 65, No. 5. https://doi.org/10.1002/asi.23028**

An altogether different view on the properties of a good performance measure than that given in E gghe (2012) is offered. E gghe argued that a good impact measure should reward nonconsistency; that is, the more citations over papers are unequally distributed, the higher the impact should be. Here, a quantitative proxy for consistency is offered, and it is shown that as consistency increases, the ideal performance measure, which is sensitive to changes in consistency, should increase, reflecting this virtue.
