### Summary
# **K. Mark Haslett (2009). Canada, Copyright, and the Common Good. *portal: Libraries and the Academy*, Vol. 9, No. 3. https://doi.org/10.1353/pla.0.0054**

Advocacy on public policy issues such as copyright is a common concern of the Association of Research Libraries (ARL) and the Canadian Association of Research Libraries (CARL). Duane Webster has been instrumental in facilitating a collaborative approach to such advocacy. Part of this involves concerted efforts to ensure that fair use (United States of America) and fair dealing (Canada) are not narrowed in a digital era. In Canada, a focus on the common good bolsters this work. Recent decisions of the Supreme Court of Canada ( Théberge 2002 and CCH 2004) have laid the groundwork for the progressive exercise and exploration of fair dealing rights in Canada.
