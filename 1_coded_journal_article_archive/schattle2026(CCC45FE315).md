# **Schattle, S. & O’Connor, J (2026). Books and Barriers: The Influence of Book Censorship on the Role of School Librarians, Their Self-Efficacy, and Well-Being. *School Library Research*, Vol. 29.**

This phenomenological study examined the impact of book censorship on the roles of school librarians within a large Texas school district. Eleven school librarians participated in in-depth individual interviews that were guided by two central research questions. The analysis revealed several emerging themes, indicating that book censorship is exerting a detrimental influence on school librarians. The findings of the study suggest that collection development is demanding significantly more time and effort than in the past, and that book censorship is adversely affecting the overall well-being of school librarians, contributing to heightened stress and challenges in their work environment.
