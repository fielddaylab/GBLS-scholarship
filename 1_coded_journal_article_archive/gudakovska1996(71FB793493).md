### Summary
# **Iveta Gudakovska (1996). The Role of Books and Reading in the Education Process in Latvia. *School Libraries Worldwide*, Vol. 2, No. 1. https://doi.org/10.29173/slw6998**

The research group of the Department of Library Science and Information of the University of Latvia surveyed Latvian students aged 10-13 years, during the teaching years 1993-1994, to determine the priorities of the young generation in today's information environment. The participants from grades 5-7 were from various Latvian schools in towns as well as country regions. A total of 440 questionnaires (from 265 girls and 175 boys) were suitable for computer analysis. The results of their inquiry included the interest areas of greatest 5-7 students, their estimation of their knowledge in the various spheres, their sources of information, the role of books and reading in their lives, their planning of time, the influence of book selection, and the role of libraries in the reading process. The aim of this article is to report on one aspect of this study, a comparison of the place of books and reading in the education process with that of other sources of information.
