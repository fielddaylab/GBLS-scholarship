### Summary
# **Neil Grimes, Ellen Pozzi (2026). Bookji and Middle School Reading Culture: Peer Influence, Emojis, and Reading Engagement. *School Libraries Worldwide*, Vol. 31, No. 1. https://doi.org/10.29173/slw8825**

Using Bookji, a social media platform, researchers conducted a research study involving two school librarians and their 7th and 8th grade (ages 12-14) students at two suburban schools in New Jersey in the United States. The study focused on reading motivation, reading engagement, school library usage, students’ social emotional connection to their reading experiences using emojis, and the influence of geographic awareness on student engagement in reading. Findings indicated that Bookji impacted students’ reading motivation, reading engagement, students’ social emotional connection to reading and the influence of geography on their reading choice. Overall, Bookji fostered a supportive and social peer-to-peer online reading culture.
