# **Dionne, K., & The Arcane Library. (2024). *LSHM: Shadowdark RPG Series*. Google Docs.**

This Low Stress High Mayhem handout supports a multi-session GameRT stream series for *Shadowdark RPG* by Kelsey Dionne and The Arcane Library. The document frames *Shadowdark* as an intuitive bridge between fifth-edition Dungeons & Dragons players and the Old School Renaissance, emphasizing dungeon exploration, torchlight, old-school risk, familiar fantasy adventure, and modernized quality-of-life features. It links to VODs for three streamed sessions and a Q&A.

The resource list is organized as a broad discovery and preparation packet. It identifies *Shadowdark RPG*, links to the Kickstarter campaign, and notes the published adventure used for the stream, *Puzzle Dungeon: The Seers' Sanctum*. It also lists live-play examples, an interview with Kelsey Dionne, articles on moving D&D players to new systems and understanding OSR play, and play-alikes across tabletop and video games. The play-alikes include *Knave*, *Mausritter*, *Rouge Second Edition*, *Ten Candles*, *Troika!*, *Trophy*, *Vast in the Dark*, *Dungeon Solitaire*, *A Plague Tale*, *Elden Ring*, and *Darkest Dungeon*.

The handout also extends advisory work across media. Read-alikes and view-alikes include fantasy novels, classic adventures, manga, anime, and animated fantasy series, while the helpful-links section gathers resources on session zero, character creation, and game mastering for different kinds of players. This makes the item more than a game record: it is a preparation scaffold for a streamed tabletop campaign, helping viewers and potential facilitators understand genre, system expectations, tone, adjacent media, and practical tools.

The facilitation and safety infrastructure is prominent. The document links to GameRT session-zero materials, guides for running session zero, D&D Beyond and Sly Flourish resources, James D'Amato character guides, Johnn Four roleplaying tips, game-master guides, Adventuring Academy interviews, *Script Change*, lines and veils, *The Door Is Open*, the TTRPG Safety Toolkit, and *Consent in Gaming*. It also includes GameRT Twitch policy language about respectful conduct, no hate speech, moderator support, harassment assistance, and private contact.

For the larger GBLS project, this source is useful as a practitioner artifact showing how a library-adjacent gaming group can scaffold a longer streamed TTRPG series. It demonstrates how advisory, collection-like pathways, staff preparation, player onboarding, session-zero norms, safety tools, and cross-media recommendations can be packaged around one program. It is especially relevant to practical planning for recurring online tabletop programs or system-introduction series.

This source should remain outside the main literature review. It is not scholarship, not an evaluation, and not a detailed case study of library implementation. It may be valuable for a practical appendix on TTRPG program packets, OSR/D&D advisory language, streamed series design, or safety and onboarding resources, but it should not be cited as evidence for GBLS outcomes.
