# **Howitt, G. (2023). *LSHM: TTRPG Honey Heist*. Google Docs.**

This short Low Stress High Mayhem handout supports a streamed GameRT session of Grant Howitt's one-page RPG *Honey Heist*. The document links to the Twitch VOD, identifies the game and creator, points viewers to a quick how-to-play video, gathers actual-play examples, and lists play-alikes, read-alikes, watch-alikes, safety tools, GameRT links, Twitch policy, and a simple stream sequence.

The advisory material frames *Honey Heist* through heist, improvisational, and caper-adjacent media. The play-alikes include *Blades in the Dark*, *Fiasco*, and *City of Mist*. The read-alikes and watch-alikes connect the game to heist fiction, art-crime nonfiction, ensemble caper film and television, and anime. This gives the handout a clear player-advisory function: it translates a one-page comic RPG into a broader discovery path for patrons or viewers who want similar tones, plots, and media experiences.

The handout also keeps facilitation infrastructure visible. It includes *Script Change*, *The Door Is Open*, and the TTRPG Safety Toolkit, along with a GameRT Twitch policy that establishes respectful conduct, bans hate speech, authorizes moderator intervention, and gives reporting/contact options. The stream summary is concise: tech check, introduction and rules, actual play, and discussion. Even in this abbreviated form, the document shows that the streamed program was framed as a moderated, facilitated event rather than an informal play session alone.

For the larger GBLS project, this item is useful as a practitioner artifact for game advisory and online tabletop programming. It shows how a library-adjacent game community can connect a lightweight RPG to how-to-play resources, actual-play culture, read/watch/play-alike recommendations, safety tools, and community norms. That model could inform practical guidance for libraries building small TTRPG programs, especially where staff need to help patrons understand a game's tone quickly.

This source should remain outside the main literature review. It is a primary resource handout, not scholarship, not a program evaluation, and not a sustained case report. It may support later appendix work on advisory records, streaming policies, safety-tool bundling, or low-preparation TTRPG facilitation, but it should not be cited as evidence for GBLS impact or outcomes.
