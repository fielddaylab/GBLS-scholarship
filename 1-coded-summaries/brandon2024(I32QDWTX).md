### Summary
# **Brandon, R., Costello, D., Porter, M., & Strang, R. (2024). RTRT - Zines - Resource Document. *Google Docs*. https://docs.google.com/document/d/1XFpG96vwsOzRS7rcTeqKx6FK15EwkI6A5B0gvtf7Qv0/edit?usp=sharing&usp=embed_facebook**

This item is a five-page Round Table Round Table resource document for a session on Zine Month, Zine Quest, tabletop RPG zines, zine-making resources, and TTRPG safety tools. The document lists panelists Russell Brandon, Danielle Costello, Molly Porter, and Rebecca Strang, all of whom bring library, teaching, programming, GameRT, game collection, board game night, Dungeons & Dragons, or youth gaming experience to the topic. It then gathers links rather than presenting a sustained article, study, or program report.

The first substantive section introduces Zine Quest and Zine Month as tabletop RPG zine movements, pointing to Kickstarter, Zine Month, Crowdfundr, and explanatory articles from Dicebreaker, Tabletop Gaming, and Medium. The document then lists current and past RPG zines such as *Beak, Feather, & Bone*, *Rolling with the Youth*, *Stargems*, *Wandlore*, *Mazes RPG*, *No Nazis in Valhalla*, *Outliers*, and many others. This makes the sheet useful for librarians who want to understand small-press RPG publishing, tabletop design culture, and zine-based game materials.

The resource also includes non-gaming zine resources from ZineLibraries.info, Vice, multiple academic-library LibGuides, and museum or university zine-making guides. These links emphasize that zines are already a library collection, preservation, programming, and instruction format, even outside games. For GBLS, that matters because RPG zines connect game creation, self-publishing, fandom, local authorship, analog making, and collection development. Zine-making can function as a game-design workshop format or as a low-cost path into tabletop RPG creation.

The most directly GBLS-relevant portion is the safety-tools section. It states that TTRPGs can cover a wide variety of topics and themes and that safety tools help participants pause, take breaks, and address uncomfortable situations. The linked tools include Script Change, Lines and Veils, The Door is Open, the TTRPG Safety Toolkit and guide, and *Consent in Gaming*. The document also includes the GameRT Twitch policy, which sets expectations for kindness, respect, anti-hate-speech moderation, harassment reporting, and private messaging.

Despite those useful pointers, this item should not be added to `review.md`. It is a resource sheet, not a piece of literature that documents a library zine program, analyzes RPG zine collections, evaluates a game-design workshop, or studies TTRPG safety tools in library settings. It is best understood as professional scaffolding for future program design. If the GBLS project later includes an appendix or toolkit, this document could help identify materials for RPG zine workshops, self-publishing events, TTRPG collection development, or safety-policy preparation. In the main literature review, stronger sources should carry the claims about TTRPG programming, game creation, inclusion, and safety.
